ECTOPIA

A blueprint for the future of civilization

By Hilal Agil

1. Modular Design

Ectopia’s modular architectural system is made up of individual components that can be used to represent any formation. The components can be used individually or combined to form everything from houses, villages, atolls to entire states. Ectopia can be integrated into an existing government system or setup as an autonomous governing state.

1.1. State

The Ectopian state formation will be inspired by the Maldives. The state would be broken down into atolls, each atoll encompassing of village islands.

1.2. Atoll

The state will be made up of islands grouped as atolls. A state can contain any number of atolls and an atoll can be made up of any number of islands.

1.3. Village

Each village will support a total population of 152 residents. This number draws reference from Dunbar’s Number which suggests that the cognitive limit to the number of people with whom one can maintain a stable social relationship is 150. A village will support a maximum of 36 housing units.

1.4. Housing Unit

A basic housing unit can support social units of 4 each. The houses will be facing the beachfront and arranged around a courtyard. The housing structure will be modular and can be configured based on the requirements.

2. Engineered for Resilience

2.1. Resilient to natural disasters

The structures will be built to be able to withstand any kind of natural disasters. 71% of the earth is the ocean. This gives us plenty of unused space to utilize for building more sustainable habitats. I see floating structures as the best option. Land reclamation means severe consequences for the environment.

2.2. Floating Structures

The floating structures will be made out of steel or concrete, anchored using mooring or using a more modern system. Springs or some other technology has to be used to keep the structures stable. Artificial reefs can be installed around and below the structure to help coral growth and support marine life to thrive.

2.3. Artificial Beaches & Lagoons

These lagoons will be similar to artificial lakes without a direct connection to the sea, however seawater will be pumped and circulated in the lagoon system.

3. Autonomous Transportation

3.1. Hyperloop System

The Ectopian state will be connected to the mainland through a Hyperloop System. The Tube will be designed to be able to float underwater.

3.2. Zero Emission Cars & Trucks

Incase the mainline is located within close proximity of the Ectopian state, Hyperloop may not be a viable option. Floating underwater tunnels will be constructed as an alternative. Zero emission cars and trucks will be used for transportation through these tunnels.

3.3. Zero Emission Boats

Zero Emission boats will be used to transport goods from the Ectopian state to the mainland.

Recreational boats may be used by the residents for leisure and if sea transportation is preferred over air. All boats within the Ectopian ecosystem will be powered by zero emission technologies.

3.4. Drones

Autonomous passenger drones will be used to transport residents within the city. These aircrafts will be zero emission, with shorter flight times and powered by battery energy.

Autonomous drones will be used to transport packages, food and other goods to and from households. These drones can be also used to transport objects within the city.

3.5. Utility Robots

Autonomous robots will be used to perform tasks like delivering goods, maintenance (such as cleaning), and other utility tasks. These robots can have wheels or legs.

3.6. Agricultural Robots

Farms will be run without relying on human labor. These farms will be more efficient, cheaper to run and produce higher quality results. Robots, drones, and autonomous tractors are some of the examples of such equipment. Planting seeds, cloud seeding, weed control, harvesting, soil analysis, monitoring, spraying, pruning are some examples of tasks these robots can perform.

4. Sustainable

4.1. Renewable Energy

A centralized energy grid for each city will be setup on a satellite island connected to each city. A combination of solar panels, wind turbines, wave energy and battery storage will be used to generate, store and distribute energy required by the city.

This setup can be configured depending on the geographical location eg: Dependence on solar energy could be reduced incase of regions with limited sunlight.

4.2. Zero Waste Systems

Each village island will be equipped with a centralized waste collection and distribution facility. Autonomous vehicles will transport the recycled materials to an industrial island to be used for the production of goods.

4.3. Fish and plant based produce

A satellite island would be used for farming and processing of fish. This promotes a healthier lifestyle and diet. Soilless and indoor farming will make it possible for the farms to produce food in any environment.

Autonomous vehicles will supply the amount required for each household in the village. The farms would share the island with forests used for harvesting sustainable building materials like bamboo.

4.4. Manufacturing with recycled materials

Industrial islands will be placed at atoll and state level. These islands will house factories, offices and other centers for conducting business and manufacturing goods. Recycled materials collected from the villages will be delivered to these islands to be used for manufacturing.

4.5. Nature reserves

Smaller satellite islands will be attached to village islands. These are ecosystems that support plant and animal life. The system will be treated the same as other protected territories.

5. Technologically Advanced

5.1. Satellite Network Constellation

Network connectivity will be made through a megaconstellation. A number of different companies are in the process of developing such networks and are expected to be operational in the near future. This removes the limitations posed by the current framework of submarine cables.

Being connected through a megaconstellation gives the islands complete location independence. Satellite internet access can’t be wiped out by bad weather. However, cell towers and fiber optic cables currently being used for providing connectivity can be knocked offline during storms.

5.2. Administrative system built on A.I

The system will be completely paperless, removing most of the bureaucratic red tape and making resource distribution more efficient. This system can be fully autonomous or work under an existing governing system.

The system will be decentralized and built on blockchain. Transportation, waste management, identity management, resource distribution will be handled through this system.

6. Utopian social design

6.1. Skill based society

The society will rely on a skill-based societal model where each person’s value within the community is tied to what they can do and what their skillset is. This will be a society more centered around promoting entrepreneurship over jobs. The members of the society will generate revenue for what they produce and will not be paid fixed salaries.

6.2. Community decision making

A fully decentralized administrative system will let every member of the society have a say within the community. Having access to a more efficient digital system makes decision making more accessible to members of the community.

6.3. Social Benefits Guarantee

Community members will be encouraged to give back to the society by contributing their skills and will risk their reputation within the community if they fail to do so. While each member of the community gets basic social benefits, old age and disability categories will be entitled to special benefits.

6.4. Privacy and Security

In a well planned society, security will not require extreme levels of policing. Security will be handled by robots and drones, without relying on surveillance that could invade the privacy of community members.

6.5. Project based education

The education system will be based on project based learning. This concept involves a more dynamic approach to learning where the students are engaged with real-world problems.

6.6. Personalized healthcare

Hospitals will use advanced technology, more personalized care and without the financial burdens we have today. Medical procedures will rely more on robotics, leaving humans to provide care and human interaction.

6.7. Healthy living environments

Areas will be allocated for recreation, wellness, ecotourism, aquatic leisure, socializing and other activities that can help a healthy community to thrive.

6.8. Central islands

Central islands will be placed at atoll and state level for social and community gatherings. The higher up the community members navigate, the more their social circles can expand. These islands will house public squares, social centers, health centers, education centers and other institutions required for the community to function.

7. Makes economical sense

7.1. The Economy

All goods and services will be made available to the members of the community without the need of money or credits. However, each member has a debt that needs to be fulfilled to cover the costs for the initial development of the infrastructure.

The debt is paid back automatically through income generated by resources like agriculture that contributes to the economy. Paying off this debt can be accelerated through contributions made to the economy either by manufacturing goods or providing services.

7.2. Exports

Agricultural produce and fish surplus will be exported to the mainland or other markets. Ectopia’s farms being contained inside controlled environments guarantees year round produce regardless of the climate and weather conditions.

Products made from sustainable and recycled materials will make up the additional exports. Scientific research, education, and professional services can be provided to gain more income for the community.

7.3. Financing

Private Developers: Loans from Banks, private investors, hedge funds.

Social Housing Projects: Can be financed by governments, non-profit organizations or the combination of both, under installment plans.

7.4. One time investment

The self-sustainable nature of the ecosystem enables it to be run continuously by relying on resources like recycled materials and renewable energy generated from within without the need for materials sourced from outside.

7.5. Guaranteed return on investment

The combination of agricultural produce, fishing and manufacturing can generate a significant income for the state.

7.6. Business continuity planning

A circular economy is possible in this situation given the fact that the economy’s reliance on the outside world is minimal. This can be taken advantage of and avoid situations like a recession.
